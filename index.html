Where Are My Files can help you make sure the answer is Yes

The "Where Are My Files?" service will list and fingerprint all the files you have on your current computers, your old computers, your external hard drives, and anything else.

The service can then tell you where your files are by filename, and where you have duplicates.

It can help you ensure all your old files are copied to a central fileserver, or just to your newest computer.

FAQ

What else can it do?
With enough development funding, WAMF can help you distinguish between your own files and those of the operating system (Windows, Mac OS-X, etc)
How will it index my files?
You will be able to download a small simple indexing program that will examine your hard drive(s) and send fingerprints to our servers.
What information will WAMF store?
Where Are My Files will store the name you give to your computer, external hard drive, or CD/DVD/USB Key. It will also store the name, folder hierarchy, size, and fingerprint of each file on your computer/external drive/etc.
How are you fingerprinting my files?
We'll use the MD5 hash. While it may be more prone to collsions than SHA-1 or other hash methods, it can often be faster to compute on older machines, and is still reasonably unique
